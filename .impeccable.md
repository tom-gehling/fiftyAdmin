# .impeccable.md — The Weekly Fifty

## Design Context

### Users
Two overlapping audiences sharing the same platform:
- **Admin team**: Content creators managing quizzes, venues, users, and submissions. Task-oriented but not highly technical — need efficiency without feeling like they're in an enterprise tool.
- **Customers/Members**: Online quiz participants accessing weekly content, archives, and Fifty+ exclusives. They arrive for the fun, not the admin.

Both groups need an interface that feels inviting — not like a backoffice system, not like a developer tool.

### Brand Personality
**3 words: Friendly · Fun · Fashionable**

The Weekly Fifty is pub trivia brought online — social by nature, light-hearted in tone, but polished enough to feel premium. Think the energy of a great pub night, not a corporate training portal.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark mode primary. The darker sage green (`#677c73`) is the key brand identifier — not the neon green accent.
- **References**: Stripe (dark) and Claude (dark) — sophisticated dark backgrounds, excellent typography, purposeful use of color, clean hierarchy, premium without cold.
- **Social media energy**: Content-forward layouts, human warmth, discoverable. Not dashboard-heavy. Think Instagram/X dark mode — not Grafana.
- **Anti-references**: Overly technical (no data-grid-first layouts, no terminal aesthetic, no monospace as personality). Not Duolingo-cute. Not enterprise SaaS grey.
- **Color**: Deep dark backgrounds (not pure black), sage green `#677c73` as the signature accent, neon green `#4cfbab` used sparingly for interactive highlights only. Soft pink `#fbe2df` as a warm secondary.
- **Typography**: Should feel editorial and warm. Lato is serviceable but generic — future work should explore more distinctive pairings. Avoid monospace as an aesthetic choice.

### Design Principles
1. **Social first, admin second** — The interface should feel like a destination people want to visit, not a tool they have to use.
2. **Warmth over precision** — Prefer rounded, human, approachable choices over sharp, technical, clinical ones.
3. **Sage green as anchor** — The dark muted green is the brand signature. Use it purposefully as the primary accent in dark mode, not the neon.
4. **Stripe-level polish** — Clean hierarchy, generous white space (or dark space), typography that earns its place. Every element should look intentional.
5. **No dashboard clichés** — Avoid hero metric layouts, identical card grids, and data-heavy patterns unless data is genuinely the point.
